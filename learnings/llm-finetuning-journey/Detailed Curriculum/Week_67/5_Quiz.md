# Week 67 Quiz — Technical Report Writing

## Multiple Choice

**Q1.** You report that your model achieves 83.1% on your 200-example TimescaleDB benchmark. A reviewer asks: "Is this benchmark evaluation contaminated?" What does this question mean and what information must your paper provide to address it?

A. The reviewer suspects your model was trained on the benchmark examples; you must show the benchmark was created after training data was frozen and that benchmark examples do not appear in training data
B. The reviewer suspects you used a biased sample; you must show the examples were randomly drawn from a larger pool
C. The reviewer suspects your metric is wrong; you must provide an alternative metric
D. The reviewer suspects your evaluation code has a bug; you must publish the evaluation script

**Q2.** You are writing the dataset statistics table. Your SFT dataset has 25,500 examples, but 18,432 were generated by a GPT-4o-mini teacher model. A reviewer is likely to raise which concern about this data?

A. GPT-4o-mini outputs are protected by copyright and cannot be used for training
B. Synthetic data from a closed-source model may introduce distribution shift or errors that propagate into the student model's behavior
C. 18,432 examples is too many; LIMA showed 1,000 examples suffice
D. The teacher model is too small to generate high-quality SQL

**Q3.** Your abstract is 210 words. The venue you are targeting has a 150-word abstract limit. Which content should you cut first?

A. The key results numbers — they take the most space
B. The motivating problem description — the audience already knows text-to-SQL is hard
C. The list of released artifacts — that belongs in the conclusion
D. The methodology description — keep only the results

**Q4.** You cite GPT-4o achieving 79.4% on your benchmark. However, you ran GPT-4o evaluations in December 2024 and the model has since been updated. How should you handle this in the paper?

A. Remove the GPT-4o comparison entirely
B. Report the result with the model version and date: "GPT-4o (gpt-4o-2024-11-20, evaluated December 2024)"
C. Replace with Claude 3.5 results since those are more reproducible
D. Average results across three GPT-4o evaluation runs from different months

## Short Answer

**Q5.** Explain the difference between a "contribution" and a "finding" in a technical report, and give one example of each from your postgres-sqlcoder-7b work.

**Q6.** Your dataset section says "we filtered low-quality examples using an LLM judge." A reproducibility reviewer says this is insufficient. List four specific details you must add.

**Q7.** You want to include a comparison to SQLCoder-7B in your paper. You did not run SQLCoder-7B on your custom 200-example benchmark — you only have its published Spider 1.0 score (80.2%). How should you handle this in your evaluation section?

## Deep Scenario

**Q8.** You submit your technical report to an ML preprint server. Three days later, a reader emails pointing out that your dataset section claims 25,500 SFT examples but your released dataset on HuggingFace has 24,817. The discrepancy is 683 examples.

Write a 150-word response that: (a) explains the most likely cause of this discrepancy, (b) describes what you must do to the paper and the dataset release, and (c) proposes a process you will use for future papers to prevent this class of error.

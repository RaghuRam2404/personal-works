# Week 70 Glossary — Technical Report: Limitations and Publication

**Limitations section**: A required section documenting specific known gaps in a paper's scope, evaluation, or methodology; distinct from future work (which describes directions forward).

**Reproducibility appendix**: A structured supplement documenting all information needed to reproduce the reported results: hyperparameters, compute, data, model, and evaluation details.

**NeurIPS reproducibility checklist**: A standardized set of yes/no questions used by NeurIPS reviewers and authors to assess whether a paper's experimental claims are reproducible.

**arXiv**: The public preprint server where most ML papers are self-published before or after peer review; accepts submissions in cs.CL, cs.LG, and cs.AI categories.

**Endorser (arXiv)**: An existing arXiv author in the relevant category who vouches for a new submitter without institutional affiliation.

**Calibration**: The degree to which a model's confidence (log-probability) matches its actual accuracy; a well-calibrated model's 90% confidence predictions are correct 90% of the time.

**Future work**: A section describing specific research directions that the current paper does not address; should include mechanism, compute estimate, and success metric — not wishes.

**Related work**: A paper section situating the contribution relative to prior work, organized by research cluster (e.g., text-to-SQL models, instruction tuning, RLHF).

**Preprint**: A paper version posted publicly before peer review; indexed by arXiv or available as a PDF on HuggingFace Hub.

**Model card**: A HuggingFace Hub README documenting a model's intended use, training details, evaluation results, and limitations; the model card can link to or embed the full technical report.
